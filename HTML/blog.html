What are the advantages and disadvantages of technology on bonsai plants?
28th January, 2023
Arpit Gupta

Bonsai, not being an inherent of technology has a fair par corners and stubbles of advantages and disadvantages . On the bright side, technology can help in automating and streamlining bonsai tree maintenance. Watering systems, for instance, can assist in ensuring that the flora are receiving the appropriate amount of water at the appropriate time. In order to enhance growth and health, technology may also be used to monitor and adjust the daylight and temperature conditions in a bonsai growing environment.

Process automation such as watering, fertilizing, and trimming with technology can help to guarantee that bonsai plants can get proper care and attention they need. It has allowed bonsai farmers and retailers to promote and sell their plants online, which can reach a wider audience and increase revenues.

Technology can also result in an over-reliance on artificial methods of preservation and care, which is a disadvantage. For example, if a bonsai tree is being grown inside a controlled environment using artificial lighting, it might not gain the same durability and strength as a tree grown in the nature. The over-pruning and over-styling of bonsai trees that can result from technology can also decrease their natural beauty and aesthetic appeal.

Growers who rely too heavily on technology can find it challenging to react to unexpected problems or changes in the environment. Small-scale growers might not be able to afford the high cost of technology and machinery. Cybersecurity threats including hacking, data breaches, and fraud may be present for bonsai producers and dealers as a result of online sales and marketing.

In conclusion, technology can have a positive or negative impact on bonsai plants' quality of life and ability to flourish. On the one hand, technology can be utilized to monitor and alter the growing environment to maximize growth and health as well as automate and simplify the care and maintenance of bonsai trees. Technology can also be utilized to enhance production, and internet marketing and sales can increase profitability. Nevertheless, technology can also result in an over-reliance on artificial maintenance and care methods, excess trimming and styling, high expenses, and cybersecurity issues. In order to maintain bonsai plants' natural beauty and aesthetic appeal while ensuring their health and vitality, it is crucial to use technology properly and in proportion.

Beginners guide for bonsai and it's benefit
10th January, 2023
Prashansa Lama

Bonsai is the living craftmanship from human beings to nature. There's nothing more intriguing than a miniature of your favorite tree in your favorite spot. It's an indulgent that we believe everyone deserves, sappy as it gets yet nothing better than it. (gardenerspath.com)

Because bonsai rather seems to be an eastern thing, newbies often get themselves pondering about the bonsai they should purchase. And sometimes taking risk is just not worth it.

Bonsai is more than a decorative miniature tree and it requires more than just watering and sunshine than your usual plants. Every aspect should be carefully considered, even decorations fearing few decorative could have properties against bonsai's needs.

Ficus Bonsai is undeniably the easiest among all to care for. Ficus is unbothered of low humidity and is very resilient, making the best for the beginners. Plus it survives equally well indoors. (bonsaiempire.com)

Chinese Elm is being not too expensive and semi-evergreen tree, it can't be out of the bonsai for beginners list. A wide pot enough for root growth and comparatively cheaper price makes it a bucket list for beginners.

Juniper even better, is an evergreen specie, a bonsai classic. This outdoor bonsai doesn't have a broadset leaf which infact looks quite fine far from wild image even without proper trim for days. Thus, the aesthetic remains unaffected without looking barbaric. (allthingsbonsai.co.uk)

Lastly, Baby Jade is an excellent bonsai for beginners. It having water fleshy, succulent and oily body parts reduces transpiration. So, you are good to go with Baby jade despite the lack of proper watering habits.
